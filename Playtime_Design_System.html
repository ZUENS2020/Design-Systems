Glass-mode order, then after-hours release

Rigorous by default. Comedic by permission.

Playtime begins in the language of transit halls, office partitions, and civic directories. Structure is precise, labels are calm, and color is used sparingly until the interface deliberately enters a warmer, more social register.

Foundations

Glass mode uses bleached neutrals and structured spacing to evoke modernist office surfaces. Royal Garden mode flips only the semantic roles, producing a warmer social atmosphere without changing the underlying component geometry.

Semantic color

Glass mode keeps the interface cool, neutral, and infrastructural.

Royal Garden shifts the same layouts into cream, gold, and plum for choreographed release.

Typography

Large headlines stay soft-edged and human. Mono labels provide the directory, gate, and room-number logic that anchors the system in modern public space.

Spacing

The grid is polite and measured. Most spacing steps stay in the middle of the scale so screens feel planned rather than expressive.

Components

Components should feel like signage furniture: modular, quiet, and subtly performative. Interactions reveal wit through timing and contrast, not novelty chrome.

Motion

Motion should drift, align, and occasionally hesitate. It is never athletic. It is choreographed around human pacing, glances through glass, and delayed comedic payoff.

Entry timing

Cards slide and fade with restrained ease. A small delay between siblings creates the sensation of spaces coming online one bay at a time.

Mode shift

Theme changes should feel like the city lights warming up after office hours, not like a system reboot.

Interaction tone

Hover states are mostly contrast and blur adjustments. If motion becomes the headline, the system has slipped out of character.

Iconography

Icons borrow from public directories, gates, arrows, elevators, and floor plans. Each mark should remain legible through glass, at distance, and in peripheral vision.

Voice & Tone

Copy sounds like a meticulous directory clerk with dry observational wit. It is never hyperactive, never startup-casual, and never overexplains the joke.

Do

Proceed to Hall C. The lift is slow, but reliable. The terrace opens at six.

Do not

Unlock an electrifying premium journey through the most vibrant mobility destination in the city.

Rule

Write like infrastructure first, then let humor arrive through timing and detail.
